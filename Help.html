Understanding Your CITIZENSHIP



What does this mean for ME?

There are 3 types of citizens in America: Native-born, Foreign-born, and Naturalized . People become citizens by being born in the United States to citizen parents, or going through a process of naturalization. This legal process has various stages to help someone obtain a new American nationality. As an immigrant, in order to receive proper access to services like healthcare, voting rights, and social security credits, it is crucial to began the process of naturalization. You can learn more about naturalization at: US Citizenship and Immigration Services. For personalized information with resources to help you begin this process, sign up for our email mailing list on our Contact page.












Understanding Your FINANCES


What does this mean for ME?

Moving to a new country in hopes of building a better life is one of the most challenging things someone could do. There are so many things to become accustomed to in a new country, the most important of which is finance. Studies have shown that foreign-born US citizens are less likely to use financial services available in the US, such as savings accounts, loans, and mortgages. As an immigrant, being weary of unforeseen territory is understandable. However, Info4Immigrants is dedicated to providing safe, and easy to understand resources about financial aid to those who need it. Read the information below to access some resources.
